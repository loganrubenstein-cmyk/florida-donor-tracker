- Data integrity bugs ship first — a reporter catching a wrong number destroys trust permanently
- "Quick wins" (one-line fixes with wide reach) ship in the same batch as big changes when they're adjacent
- Every phase has a verification step (Puppeteer screenshot + SQL check where applicable)

## Phase 6 — Profile depth (cross-cutting — "pack every page")

**Principle:** Every profile is a piece of public-records journalism. A reporter, voter, or political pro should leave knowing *everything the database can tell them* about this entity, with sources and methodology one click away. No blank tabs, no placeholder "—", no surface-only summaries when we hold richer data in Supabase.

**For every profile page, audit against this checklist:**
1. **Identity block** — every available ID (acct_num, FEIC/IRS EIN if known, DOE slug, House/Senate people_id), entity type badge, status (active/closed/revoked + effective date), years active span.
2. **Top-line stats** — totals with cycle breakdown, rank among peers ("#X of Y in 2022 cycle"), YoY delta.
3. **Time-series chart** — quarterly or annual bars, YAxis labeled, hover tooltip, legend.
4. **Relationships** — who this entity is connected to and how (shared treasurer/chair/address, common donors, common vendors, lobbyist↔principal, firm↔lobbyist, committee↔candidate linkage).
5. **Top-N tables** — top donors, top recipients, top bills, top principals, top lobbyists, top clients — each with a "View all" deep link to `/explorer` or the relevant directory.
6. **Cross-type links** — a committee profile should link to its linked candidates, the cycle page, the industry of its top donor, and the lobbyists who represent its donors (where data exists).
7. **In the News** block — `NewsBlock` wired to a keyword query for this entity.
8. **DataTrustBlock** — always at the bottom: source, source URL, last updated, direct/normalized/inferred columns, caveats specific to this entity type.
9. **Methodology pointer** — one line: "How we calculated these numbers → /methodology#<anchor>".
10. **Verification link** — "View on FL Division of Elections / FL House Lobbyist Portal / LegiScan" — per `source_links_for_frontend.md`.

### 6.1 Donor profile (`/donor/[slug]`)
**Add (from contributions table — data already present):**
- **Occupation + employer strip** — top 5 self-reported occupations/employers across the donor's filings, with counts. (Data: `contributions.occupation`, `contributions.employer`.)
- **Recipient mix donut** — hard vs soft vs party vs candidate-direct split.
- **Cycle chart** — total giving by cycle-year, colored by party of recipient.
- **Top recipients table (top 25, currently top 10)** with party, office, cycle per row; click → candidate/committee profile.
- **Geographic strip** — donor's reported ZIP(s) → county → link to `/district/[county]`.
- **"Other donors at same address/employer"** — neighbor cluster from `/connections` data.
- **Linked PACs** — committees where this donor is the dominant funder (>25% share) per `/decode` logic.
- **Raw filings table** — every line item, deep-linked to `/explorer?donor_slug=<slug>`.

### 6.2 Committee profile (`/committee/[acct_num]`)
**Add:**
- **Registration panel** — treasurer, chair, registered address, PAC type (CCE/ECO/PCO), date registered, status, IRS 527/501c4 EIN if known (stub for now; filled when IRS 990 scraper lands).
- **Linked candidates** — from `candidate_pc_edges`; show edge type (solicitation / inferred / self-reported).
- **Top donors (25)** + giving concentration stat ("Top 10 donors = X% of receipts").
- **Recipients / expenditures** — who this committee paid; cross-link to vendor profiles if we have them.
- **Quarterly cashflow chart** — receipts vs expenditures bars.
- **Connected committees** — shared treasurer / shared address (from `/connections`).
- **IE spending** — independent expenditures filed by this PAC (when IE scrape lands).
- **"Derived" or "Closed" badge** — status-driven; explains why this committee may not appear in the FL active-registry search.

### 6.3 Candidate / politician profile (`/candidate/[acct_num]`, `/politician/[slug]`)
**Add:**
- **Career totals strip** — combined raised across ALL cycles (currently each cycle is siloed).
- **Cycle-to-cycle comparison table** — easy scan across 2018/2020/2022/2024.
- **Donor industry breakdown** (requires industry classifier completion — see `industry_classifier_todo.md`).
- **Geographic donor map** — top donor ZIPs/counties.
- **Linked PACs** — "Soft money tracked via: Friends of Ron DeSantis, Empower Parents PAC" with receipt totals each.
- **Vendors paid** — top 25 expenditure recipients (often reveals consultants, media buyers).
- **Co-fundraisers / shared donors** — candidates whose donor list overlaps ≥ X%.
- **Self-funding flag** — if candidate gave >$50K to own committee.
- **Bill / legislative tab** (for sitting legislators) — cross-link to `/legislator/[id]` for votes, sponsorships, committee assignments.

### 6.4 Lobbyist profile (`/lobbyist/[slug]`)
**Add:**
- **Principals represented (all, currently top N)** — with years and est. compensation each.
- **Bills lobbied on** — aggregated from `bill_disclosures` where lobbyist matches.
- **Compensation trend** — quarterly bars across years active.
- **Firm history** — if lobbyist appears at multiple firms across time, render as timeline.
- **Co-lobbyists at firm** — people they work alongside (from `lobbyists` table).
- **Campaign contributions** — cross-link only (per `feedback_lobbyist_page_focus.md`): "This person also appears in contribution records → /donor/[slug]".

### 6.5 Principal profile (`/principal/[slug]`)
**Add:**
- **All lobbyists + firms retained** — with years and comp each.
- **Bills lobbied on** — what this principal is spending to influence.
- **Compensation trend** chart.
- **Industry classification** — link to `/industry/[slug]`.
- **PAC giving cross-link** — if principal's name matches a contributor (`/donor/[slug]`).
- **Parent company / affiliate pointer** — if manually mapped (stub for now).

### 6.6 Lobbying firm profile (`/lobbying-firm/[slug]`)
Already covered by 4.2 + this checklist. Additional items here:
- **Rebrand history** — "Formerly Southern Strategy Group (2007–2019)" pulled from `canonical_slug` mapping (4.1).
- **Partner / name-principal flag** — per 4.3.
- **Client tenure** — for each top client, first-year / last-year of engagement.
- **Practice-area donut** — industries of the client book.

### 6.7 Cycle page (`/cycle/[year]`)
**Add:**
- **Chamber breakdown** — Senate vs House vs Statewide vs Local totals.
- **Party breakdown** — receipts by R / D / NPA / other.
- **Top PACs** — not just top candidates.
- **Top donors** — with self/combined flag.
- **Self-funders** — candidates who funded >X% from own wallet.
- **Notable races** — curated hand-picked list for marquee cycles (2018/2022/2024).
- **Methodology note** (per 3.3).

### 6.8 Industry page (`/industry/[slug]`)
**Add:**
- **Top donors in industry** — ranked, with cross-party split.
- **Top recipients** — candidates and PACs receiving most from this industry.
- **Connected legislators** — members with industry in top-3 donor sectors.
- **Top lobbyists / firms** — who this industry retains in Tallahassee.
- **Cycle trend chart** — industry giving over time.
- **Bills lobbied on** — top bills by industry principal activity.

### 6.9 Legislator page (`/legislator/[id]`)
**Add (or verify — some may exist):**
- **Committee assignments** with chair/vice flag.
- **Bills sponsored / cosponsored** — with status pills.
- **Key votes** — floor votes, especially high-profile.
- **Campaign finance cross-link** — "See contributions → /politician/[slug]".
- **District link** — `/district/[county]`.

### 6.10 Bill page (`/lobbying/bill/[slug]`)
**Add:**
- **Sponsors / cosponsors** (from LegiScan — cross-link `/legislator/[id]`).
- **Current status + history timeline** (from LegiScan).
- **Principal industry mix** — who's lobbying on this bill by sector.
- **Firm mix** — top lobbying firms filing on this bill.
- **Related bills** — same subject / companion bills.

### 6.11 Shared: `EntityHeader` + `RelationshipsBlock` components
To keep this from becoming per-page drift, build two shared primitives:
- `components/shared/EntityHeader.js` — renders identity block + top-line stats + status badges consistently across all profile types.
- `components/shared/RelationshipsBlock.js` — renders the "connected to" section with consistent visual grammar (tokens, edge-type badges, click-through links).

Roll out 6.1–6.10 *on top of* these primitives rather than reinventing each profile layout.
